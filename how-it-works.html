Analyst Assist, Built Like a Coach Tool

How Analyst Assist works

Analyst Assist turns opponent film data and live game charting into a fast tendency board. It does not claim to know the next play with certainty. It helps your staff identify what the offense is most likely to do based on situation, opponent history, live adjustments, sequence behavior, and key-player impact.

Coach workflow

1

Load opponent data

Import charted film or manually enter plays with down, distance, field zone, personnel, formation, concept, play type, result, and optional star-player status.

2

Select the current situation

During prep or live game mode, choose down, distance, zone, personnel, formation family, hash, tempo, and star status.

3

The engine evaluates likely answers

Each possible call is evaluated using matching film, matching live-game plays, broader opponent tendencies, recent sequence behavior, and key-player influence.

4

The board turns signals into percentages

The system converts those tendency signals into top plays plus overall Run, Pass, RPO, and Screen probability.

5

Coaches log what actually happened

The box coach can log detail. The sideline coach can simply tap Run %, Pass %, RPO %, or Screen % to record accountability and keep the board synced.

What the prediction is

A weighted tendency model. It is best used as a coach alert system: “what should we be ready for right now?”

Best use: identify tendency, alert the sideline, and compare film tendency vs. live tendency.

Low sample sizes should be treated as alerts, not certainties.

How the scoring works

For each possible offensive answer, Analyst Assist compares the current situation against your opponent data and live charting. It gives the strongest credit to situations that closely match what is happening right now, then blends in broader tendencies when the exact sample is small.

What matters most: down and distance, field zone, personnel, formation, live-game calls, recent sequencing, and whether a key player is on or off the field.
Coach takeaway: the board is not guessing randomly. It is organizing the strongest tendency signals into a usable game-day recommendation.

Situation matching

The model starts with the closest matching situations first. If the sample is thin, it backs out to broader buckets so the board still gives your staff a useful answer without pretending the data is stronger than it is.

How the percentages work

Once the system ranks likely offensive answers, it groups them into play-type buckets so the sideline can quickly see the bigger picture.

Run % shows how much the current situation leans toward run-family calls.
Pass % shows how much the current situation leans toward dropback or pass-family calls.
RPO % shows how often the offense may attach or package a read element to the call.
Screen % is separated because coaches often want screen alerts treated differently than normal pass game.

Live-game weighting

Live game data becomes more important as more plays are logged. Early in the game, film study still carries more of the board. As the opponent reveals its plan, the live call sheet starts to carry more influence.

Coach takeaway: the system should not overreact after two or three plays. It gets more confident as the live sample grows.

Sequence tracking

Analyst Assist watches how the offense strings calls together. It looks for patterns such as what they like after a run, what they like after a screen, or what they call after a specific concept shows up.

Coach takeaway: the alert is about the offense’s rhythm, not just the static down-and-distance tendency.

Key-player adjustment

If you chart whether a key player is on or off the field, Analyst Assist can adjust the board toward the concepts that player usually affects.

Coach takeaway: if the offense changes when a certain back, quarterback, receiver, or tight end is in the game, the board can help flag that tendency.

If there are not enough on/off samples, the model shows a low-confidence alert instead of forcing a strong adjustment.
