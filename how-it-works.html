How the Defensive Play Predictor works

The site turns opponent film data and live game charting into a fast tendency board. It does not claim to know the next play with certainty. It ranks the most likely offensive answers based on situation, opponent history, live adjustments, sequence behavior, and star-player impact.

Coach workflow

1

Load opponent data

Import charted film or manually enter plays with down, distance, field zone, personnel, formation, concept, play type, result, and optional star-player status.

2

Select the current situation

During prep or live game mode, choose down, distance, zone, personnel, formation family, hash, tempo, and star status.

3

The engine scores every active concept

Each possible call gets a weighted score from matching film, matching live-game plays, general baseline tendency, sequence boost, and star-player adjustment.

4

Scores become percentages

The system normalizes all concept scores so the board can show top plays plus overall Run, Pass, RPO, and Screen probability.

5

Coaches log what actually happened

The box coach can log detail. The sideline coach can simply tap Run %, Pass %, RPO %, or Screen % to record accountability and keep the board synced.

What the prediction is

A weighted tendency model. It is best used as a coach alert system: “what should we be ready for right now?”

Best use: identify tendency, alert the sideline, and compare film tendency vs. live tendency.

Low sample sizes should be treated as alerts, not certainties.

Main scoring formula

For each offensive concept, the engine blends baseline football logic, film matches, live-game matches, sequence history, and star-player adjustments.

concept_score = weighted_data_score + sequence_boost + star_adjustment
If no data exists:
concept_score = baseline_bias
If only film exists:
concept_score = film_match × 0.74 + baseline_bias × 0.26
If only live data exists:
concept_score = live_match × 0.74 + baseline_bias × 0.26
If film and live data both exist:
concept_score = film_weight × (film_match × 0.67 + baseline × 0.33)
+ live_weight × (live_match × 0.70 + baseline × 0.30)

Situation match formula

The model gives the most credit to plays that match the exact game situation, then backs off to broader tendency buckets.

match_score = exact_situation_rate × 0.28
+ down_distance_rate × 0.22
+ personnel_rate × 0.13
+ formation_rate × 0.13
+ phase_rate × 0.09
+ overall_rate × 0.15
Match typeMeaning
Exact situationSame down, distance bucket, and field zone.
Down-distanceSame down and distance bucket.
PersonnelSame offensive personnel grouping.
FormationSame formation or formation family.
PhaseSame game phase, such as normal, two-minute, red zone, or backed-up.
OverallOpponent’s total concept rate across the dataset.

Percentage formula

After every concept is scored, the scores are converted into percentages by dividing each score by the total score of all active concepts.

concept_percentage = concept_score ÷ sum(all_concept_scores)
Run % = sum(percentages where type = Run)
Pass % = sum(percentages where type = Pass)
RPO % = sum(percentages where type = RPO)
Screen % = sum(percentages where type = Screen)

Live-game weighting

Live game data becomes more important as more plays are logged. Early in the game, film study still carries more of the model. As the opponent reveals its plan, live tendency gets more influence.

film_weight = 1 − live_weight
live_weight increases as live logged plays increase
Example: after only a few plays, the model should not overreact. After a larger live sample, the live call sheet should start carrying the prediction.

Sequence boost

The engine looks at what the opponent tends to call after the last logged concept or after the last play type bucket.

sequence_boost = exact_previous_concept_next_rate × 0.16 × confidence
+ previous_bucket_next_rate × 0.09 × confidence

Confidence rises as the number of matching sequence samples rises.

Star-player adjustment

If you chart whether a key player is on or off the field, the model can adjust concepts that depend on that player.

star_adjustment = touch_delta × 0.26
+ run_delta × 0.12
+ screen_rpo_delta × 0.10
+ red_zone_touch_delta × 0.16
+ third_long_touch_delta × 0.20

If the star is OFF, the adjustment is reversed and reduced. If there are not enough ON/OFF samples, the model shows a low-confidence alert instead of forcing a strong adjustment.
