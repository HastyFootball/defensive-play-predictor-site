Analyst Assist, Built Like a Coach Tool

How Analyst Assist works

The site turns opponent film data and live game charting into a fast tendency board. It does not claim to know the next play with certainty. It ranks the most likely offensive answers based on situation, opponent history, live adjustments, sequence behavior, and star-player impact.

Coach workflow

1

Load opponent data

Import charted film or manually enter plays with down, distance, field zone, personnel, formation, concept, play type, result, and optional star-player status.

2

Select the current situation

During prep or live game mode, choose down, distance, zone, personnel, formation family, hash, tempo, and star status.

3

The engine scores every active concept

Each possible call gets a weighted score from matching film, matching live-game plays, general baseline tendency, sequence boost, and star-player adjustment.

4

Scores become percentages

The system normalizes all concept scores so the board can show top plays plus overall Run, Pass, RPO, and Screen probability.

5

Coaches log what actually happened

The box coach can log detail. The sideline coach can simply tap Run %, Pass %, RPO %, or Screen % to record accountability and keep the board synced.

What the prediction is

A weighted tendency model. It is best used as a coach alert system: “what should we be ready for right now?”

Best use: identify tendency, alert the sideline, and compare film tendency vs. live tendency.

Low sample sizes should be treated as alerts, not certainties.

How concepts are ranked

Analyst Assist reviews every active offensive concept and ranks it using a blend of football context, opponent history, and live-game evidence. The goal is not to expose a raw formula; the goal is to give the staff a fast, trustworthy answer before the snap.

InputHow it helps
SituationDown, distance, field zone, score, tempo, and game phase help identify what the offense usually wants in that exact moment.
PersonnelThe model compares the current personnel grouping to prior calls from the same grouping.
FormationThe model uses both exact formation and broader formation family so it can still help when tags vary.

Live dataAs the game develops, live charting becomes more important because it reflects the opponent’s current plan.

How Run, Pass, RPO, and Screen percentages are created

After concepts are ranked, Analyst Assist groups them by play type. That gives coaches a clean sideline view: how likely is the offense to run, pass, tag an RPO, or throw a screen from this situation?

Coach translation: the percentages are a live tendency split, not a guarantee. Use them as alerts for what your call sheet needs to defend right now.

Live-game weighting

Early in the game, Analyst Assist leans more on opponent film and baseline tendency. As more live plays are logged, it shifts toward what the offense is actually doing that night.

Example: if film said the opponent was balanced, but they open the game with repeated second-down screens, the sideline view starts surfacing that screen tendency faster.

Sequence and star-player context

The engine also watches for sequencing patterns, such as what follows a run, shot, screen, or RPO. If a key player is tagged as ON/OFF, it can adjust alerts around concepts that commonly involve that player.

When sample size is thin, Analyst Assist should be treated as a tendency alert rather than a hard prediction.
